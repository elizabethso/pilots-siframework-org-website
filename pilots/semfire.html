SEMHIE, FireStar

Southeast Michigan Health Information Exchange (SEMHIE) has an approved ONC Transition of Care (ToC) Reference Implementation (RI) Pilot. SEMHIE is using a unique, repeatable, highly flexible, cost-effective and open approach that provides an important value proposition for large health systems and the smallest providers to achieve Meaningful Use. SEMHIE, a qualified sub-state HIE in Michigan's state-level HIE, operates in a safety-net region with 5,000,000 patients, six major health systems, 14,000 physicians, and over 138 different healthcare vendor products. Providing interoperability to this diverse community is a challenging task and the driving factor for SEMHIE in developing its approach. For the ToC RI Pilot, SEMHIE uses Model Driven Architecture (MDA) with Object Management Group's (OMG) standards, Open Health Tools (OHT) open source software, Discharge Summary ToC standards developed by ONC S&I Framework, plus SEMHIE's NwHIN production CONNECT infrastructure from its SSA e-Disability Claims contract. SEMHIE, its SSA health systems and a physician group will demonstrate at HIMSS how members get interoperability with a compelling value proposition. Additionally, SEMHIE's work to produce the solution will be placed in the OHT open source MDHT project, available to all who wish to leverage the SEMHIE ToC Reference Implementation Pilot approach and the software produced.
