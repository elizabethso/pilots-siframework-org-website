LRI Pilot ABC

MyHealth Access is teaming up with their eco-partners Regional Medical Labs and Covinsint to pilot and demonstrate the exchange of electronic clinical laboratory results conforming to the LRI Implementation Guide.

This pilot objective is to demonstrate the following LRI services:

  1. Exchange of electronic clinical laboratory results from LIS to ambulatory EMR
  2. EMR display of the received laboratory test results
  3. Validation conforming to the implementation guide
  4. Feedback on the Vocabulary usage (LOINC, SNOMED CT and UCUM)

Participant Benefits

  • Substantially reduce the cost and the time to implement new laboratory results in ambulatory settings
  • Provide an implementation guide for electronic reporting of ambulatory care laboratory test results
  • Support Meaningful Use Stage 1 requirements for exchange of data, by enabling incorporation of lab results into an EHR as structured data
  • Reduce the variance in HL7 v2 field details and vocabulary usage currently seen in clinical labs and EHRs
  • Harmonize message structure and vocabularies, building on existing HL7 v2 implementation guides
  • Serve as a stepping stone of a path to a single harmonized IG or closely related family of IGs that can be deployed across the full continuum (ambulatory, public health, acute, device, etc.)
  • Aid in the alignment between laboratory messages and exchange instances used in support of Transitions of Care and other primary content definitions
  • Further the goals of other Meaningful Use requirements such as quality reporting and decision support
  • Increase the prevalence of common core subsets of LOINC codes for common tests and analytes, thus reducing downstream issues in reporting and clinical data support (CDS)
  • Support Evidenced Based Medicine research initiatives such as Comparative Effectiveness Research (CER)
  • Reduce medical errors by eliminating manual entry of lab data into EHRs

How to Get Started

To get started as a prospective pilot participant:

  1. Visit the LRI Pilots Wiki (http://wiki.siframework.org/LRI+Pilots+WG) to learn more about Pilots Workgroup activities
  2. Participate in the LRI Pilots Workgroup and attend the weekly WG call
  3. Review the LRI Pilot Guide (http://wiki.siframework.org/LRI+Pilots+Guide)
  4. Call or email the ONC Support team members (Ann Clarke 443-348-2765, Ann.R.Clarke@lmco.com) to get further guidance

How to Join

To join this pilot team:

  1. Visit the LRI Pilots Wiki (To view click on the following URL: http://wiki.siframework.org/LRI+Pilots+WG) to learn more about Pilots Workgroup activities (Check this pilot charter) and Review the LRI Pilot Guide (To view click on the following URL: http://wiki.siframework.org/LRI+Pilots+Guide)
  2. Register as a committed member on the LRI Demos & Pilots Wiki
  3. Call or email the Pilot Team lead expressing your interest and to get further guidance
  4. Propose your contribution to this pilot, objectives and expectations
  5. Commit adequate resources to successfully complete the proposed scope
